ISP Detail Visualizations

Zoomed-in views of an individual ISP partner and its relationship with users, infrastructure, and the Share backbone. These are for the "For ISP Partners" page — showing what it looks like from the partner's perspective once they're on the Share network.

1

ISP Radial Network

The ISP at center, users radiating outward, Share backbone below

The ISP as the hero. A central node with users as dots orbiting around it in concentric rings (by plan tier or distance). Below, a single thick connection down to Share's backbone. Data pulses flow up from Share and radiate outward to every user. Shows the ISP's reach and how Share powers it from behind.

Why this works for ISPs

The ISP is literally at the center. They see their users around them, their revenue stats, and the single clean line down to Share. It answers: "What does my network look like on Share?" The tier rings show internet packages — something ISPs already think in terms of.

Best used for

Hero section of the ISP Partners page. The "after" state — what your network looks like once you're on Share. Can be personalized per partner in a future dashboard view. Data pulses reinforce the "alive" feeling.

2

Cell Tower Broadcast

Literal tower with signal radiating to homes — fiber feeding from below

A recognizable cell tower at center with signal waves pulsing outward. Below ground, Share's fiber backbone feeds into the base. Above, the signal reaches homes and buildings in the surrounding area. This makes the abstract tangible — ISPs see exactly the physical infrastructure they understand, powered by something new.

Why this works for ISPs

Cell towers are what ISPs actually build and operate. Seeing their physical infrastructure connected to Share's fiber — with signal reaching buildings and windows lighting up — is instantly recognizable. The underground fiber line makes the invisible backbone visible. "Powered by Share" at the base tells the story.

Best used for

A section explaining "How it works" on the ISP Partners page. Can also be used in the homepage data packet journey (Beat 4 — the last mile). The tower is a transition point between Share's world (backbone) and the ISP's world (last mile delivery).

3

Data Center Cross-Section

Share's DC interior — fiber in, multiple ISP connections out

A stylized cross-section of a Share data center. Subsea fiber enters from the left. Inside: server racks, switches, routers with blinking LEDs. From the right side, multiple ISP connection lines fan out — each labeled with an ISP name. Data packets flow through the DC, get routed, and split to the right ISPs. Shows Share as the intelligent routing layer.

Why this works for ISPs

ISPs understand data centers, racks, and routers. Seeing their name on one of the output lines — with their user count — makes the connection personal. The "SHARE ROUTER" box in the middle demystifies the coordination layer. Left side = global internet flowing in. Right side = ISP partners getting their share.

Best used for

A "How Share Works" explainer section. Shows the data center as the physical meeting point between global infrastructure and local ISPs. Good for technical visitors who want to understand the architecture. Pairs well with infrastructure photos Share is sending.

4

ISP Before / After Split Screen

Left: ISP alone. Right: ISP on Share. Same ISP, different world.

Split screen. Left side: an ISP operating alone — one thin connection to one provider, small coverage, few users, stacked costs. Right side: the same ISP on Share — thick backbone connection, expanded coverage, multiplied users, revenue flowing. The divider can be dragged or scroll-animated. The most direct "what's in it for me" visualization.

Why this works for ISPs

The most persuasive format: same ISP, two realities. Left side is their current pain. Right side is what Share offers. The revenue comparison alone (12% margin → 45% margin) does the selling. Every ISP visiting can project themselves onto this.

Best used for

Hero or above-the-fold section of the ISP Partners page. Can be interactive — slide the divider or scroll-trigger the "after" state. This is the single most conversion-oriented visualization. Pairs perfectly with a "Get Started" CTA right below.

5

ISP Neighborhood Map

City-level view — towers, fiber, buildings, users, all connected

A bird's-eye view of an ISP's actual service area. Share's fiber backbone runs through the area. Branching off: the ISP's towers and local fiber. Around each tower: buildings with active connections shown as thin lines. Users appear as warm dots inside buildings. This is the most literal "what your network looks like on Share" — a living map of a real neighborhood powered by the ecosystem.

Why this works for ISPs

ISPs think in terms of towers, coverage areas, and subscribers in buildings. This shows exactly that — their physical deployment, powered by Share's backbone running through the middle. Each lit window is a paying user. The bird's-eye view makes small feel big — a handful of towers covering a real area.

Best used for

A "Your Network on Share" section or an interactive coverage explorer. Could be powered by real data from Share's API — showing actual tower locations and user density. Also works as a future dashboard visualization for partners. The most CMS-friendly approach since locations are data-driven.
