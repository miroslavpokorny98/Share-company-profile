Direction 6 — Three Versions

Same narrative arc, three different entry points. All follow: Problem → Share as Solution → ISP Growth → Ecosystem → Africa Vision. The difference is where and how we start.

Shared Narrative Arc

  1. The Problem: An ocean of data on one side, scarcity on the other. Existing infrastructure not connected, not coordinated. Cables exist but don't talk to each other. Missing potential.
  2. Share Connects: Share enters as the coordination layer. Bundles get connected. Data floods through. Speed becomes palpable.
  3. The ISP Grows: Zoom out — that was one ISP plugging in. Their coverage expands, users multiply, revenue grows.
  4. The Ecosystem: More ISPs join. Each connection strengthens everyone. The network is dense, active, alive.
  5. Africa Connected: Full zoom out. Share has connected the continent. Subsea cables to the world. The vision.

What each audience takes away from the homepage

ISP Provider

"This solves my problem. I want to plug in, simplify my life, and grow through them."

Infrastructure Provider

"They utilize underused infrastructure that I also have. I want to be in their ecosystem."

Investor

"They don't build their own infrastructure — it won't cost billions. Smart business model."

End User

"I want blazing fast internet. This network is amazing — how can I use it?"

Government

"They help local businesses grow. They're good for Africa."

Ocean of Data

The imbalance: a sea of data on one side, near-darkness on the other

Particle cloud + fiber bundles. Data as blinking light dots/dashes.

Frustration → breakthrough → expansion → ecosystem → vision

Beat 5 — Africa Connected
Final zoom out. The entire network spans across Africa. The backbone connects coast to coast. ISPs dot the continent. Users are everywhere. Subsea cables extend to screen edges — connecting Africa to the world. Vision numbers: "1M+ users, 100K+ km fiber, 30+ data centers."
"Africa's shared backbone. Connected to the world. The future of internet infrastructure starts here."

Strengths

  • The imbalance hook is immediately visceral — anyone understands abundance vs. scarcity
  • Data as visible light/particles makes "bandwidth" tangible for non-technical visitors
  • The moment data floods through is emotionally powerful — relief + speed
  • Works for all audiences: ISPs see the problem they live, investors see the opportunity, end users see the speed
  • The progressive zoom-out is a series of revelations — each one bigger than the last

Risks

  • Particle-heavy first beat needs careful performance optimization for Android/slow connections
  • "Left vs. right" framing is more abstract — doesn't show physical infrastructure like cables and towers
  • The problem beat may feel negative for the first few seconds before the solution arrives
  • Needs very strong copy to anchor what the particles represent

Tour existing infra: cables, towers, DCs. It's all there. Impressive — until it isn't.

Infrastructure icons appearing confidently, then disconnecting/dimming. Bait and switch.

Confidence → unease → relief → growth → awe

Share's node appears at center with a powerful glow. Connection lines reach out to each infrastructure element. One by one, disconnected pieces RECONNECT through Share. Dashed lines become solid. "Share is the coordination layer. One contract. Every provider. Unlimited backhaul with no upfront commits."

"ISPs plug in and grow. More coverage. More users. More revenue. Share only earns when partners earn."

Full ecosystem view. Dozens of ISPs, dense user coverage. Financial layer appears as a subtle green pulse running alongside the data backbone — representing the payment gateway. Two systems, one ecosystem: backbone (blue) + payments (green). Both flowing together.
"Backbone and payment gateway. Two layers. One ecosystem. Growing every day."
Africa connected —

Beat 6 — Africa Connected
Final zoom to continental scale. Network maps onto Africa's shape. Subsea cables extend to screen edges. Vision numbers: "1M+ users, 100K+ km fiber, 30+ data centers." The full vision revealed — from disconnected infrastructure to a connected continent.
"Africa's shared backbone. The most advanced internet network, built from Africa for the world."

Strengths

  • The "bait and switch" — showing impressive infra then revealing it doesn't work — is memorable
  • Infrastructure providers specifically see their assets in Beat 1 and understand the opportunity
  • Most literal approach — shows real cables, towers, DCs. ISPs recognize their world.
  • The disconnect moment directly references Share's narrative: "none of it speaks to each other"
  • 6 beats gives more room for each moment to breathe
  • The financial layer (green) in Beat 5 introduces the payment gateway naturally

Risks

  • 6 beats is longer — needs to maintain engagement throughout the scroll
  • The disconnect moment might confuse first-time visitors who don't know the context
  • More complex to build — many infrastructure elements with two states each (connected/disconnected)
  • Less emotionally immediate than Version A's abundance/scarcity contrast

Fiber Perspective

Starts INSIDE a fiber cable — dark unused strands — then zooms out to the continent

Inside a fiber cable. 12 strands, only 2 are lit. The rest are dark. Wasted potential.

Micro to macro. Fiber strands as the consistent element at every scale. Cinematic zoom-outs.

Intimate wonder → frustration → satisfaction → expansion → awe

Beat 3: The frayed bundle gets BOUND together by Share. All strands light up simultaneously.

Each strand has a contract document icon. A bottleneck where capacity gets lost. Data dribbles through each independently. Fragmented. Wasteful.
"Fragmented. Every provider runs their own strand. Every ISP negotiates separate contracts. Costs stack. Capacity is wasted."

Beat 3 — Share Binds the Bundle
A glowing ring appears at the bottleneck — Share. The frayed strands are PULLED TOGETHER, bound into a unified bundle. The bottleneck opens up. Light FLOODS through — ALL strands now lit, all carrying data. The right-side bundle is now as bright and thick as the left. Deeply satisfying: chaos becomes order, dark becomes light.
"Share binds the bundle. One coordination layer. One contract. Every provider. Unlimited capacity flows through."

Pull back more. The unified bundle is actually the BACKBONE feeding into an ISP node. From the ISP, smaller cables and wireless signals reach out to users (amber dots). Coverage is large and active. Additional ISP nodes visible in the background — each connected to the same backbone.
"One ISP plugs in. Unlimited backhaul, zero upfront commits. Their coverage explodes. Their users multiply."

Beat 5 — Zoom Out: Africa
Final pull-back. Full network revealed — backbone spanning Africa-scale. Multiple ISPs with user clusters dot the landscape. Subsea cable bundles enter from the left edge. The entire continent unified, active, alive. The fiber strands from Beat 1 are now the arteries of a continental network. Vision numbers appear.
"From cable to continent. Africa's shared backbone. The most advanced internet network, built from Africa for the world."

Strengths

  • The "inside the fiber" opening is unique — no other infra company starts here. Delivers the "internet is COOL" moment Share wants.
  • Fiber strands as a consistent element at every zoom level creates visual continuity
  • The binding moment (Beat 3) is the most visually satisfying of all three versions
  • Progressive zoom-out = progressive revelation. Each beat is bigger than the last.
  • Closest to Share's own creative brief (data packet through fiber) but reframed around the problem-solution narrative

Risks

  • Abstract opening — "dark fiber strands" might not land immediately with non-technical visitors
  • Needs very strong copy to explain what the dark strands MEAN (unused capacity)
  • The zoom-out transitions are technically the hardest to execute smoothly
  • Less immediately accessible than Version A's abundance/scarcity contrast
