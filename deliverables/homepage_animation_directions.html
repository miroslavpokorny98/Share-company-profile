Homepage Animation — 5 Directions

Each direction offers a different storytelling angle and visual language for the homepage scroll experience. All are designed to land with ISPs in under 15 seconds while serving the full audience spectrum.

Design Constraints (derived from research)

15-second test

ISP visitor must understand: Share is a backbone provider, not a competitor. They should feel "I want to collaborate."

Primary emotion

Trust + scale. ISPs fear disruption and don't know Share. The animation must build credibility before it asks for anything.

Technical reality

Mostly mobile, Android, slower connections. Must feel lean and fast. Heavy 3D needs aggressive optimization or progressive loading.

All audiences, one page

ISPs (#1), infrastructure partners (#2), investors (#3), end users (#4), governments. Statement of intent, not a pitch to one buyer.

1

The Unification

From fragmentation to one network — the problem IS the visual

Satellite-view infrastructure map, glowing lines on dark terrain

Macro (Africa-wide) to micro (single connection). Chaos resolves into order.

Disorder → recognition → unity → immersion

"All this infrastructure already exists. It just needed Share."

Scroll Storyboard
Scattered infrastructure
across Africa
Beat 1 — The Problem
Dark map of Africa. Disconnected glowing lines — fiber, towers, cables — scattered everywhere. Pulsing independently. Clearly not talking to each other.
"The infrastructure exists. 7 subsea cables. 500+ km of fiber. But none of it speaks to each other."
Lines reaching toward
a central point
Beat 2 — The Tension
Lines begin reaching toward each other. Dashed connections attempt to form. A central node appears — faint at first. The network wants to exist but can't coordinate.
"600 million people unconnected. Not because the pipes aren't there — because there's no layer that makes them work as one."
Share connects —
one unified network
Beat 3 — The Solution
Share's node ignites. All lines snap into connection — solid, bright, unified. Data pulses flow. The map transforms from scattered fragments into one coherent, luminous network. The shift is dramatic and satisfying.
"Share is the coordination layer. Not a new network — the connective tissue that makes what already exists finally work as one."
Scale reveals —
numbers + density
Beat 4 — The Proof
Camera holds on the unified network. Numbers fade in: 7 subsea cables, 500+ km fiber, 10 data centers, 8M+ people. The density of activity is the proof — this is already live, already working.
"Already live. Already connecting networks across Kenya. And this is just the beginning."
Pan-African vision —
subsea to global
Beat 5 — The Vision
Pull back further. Africa's network connects outward — subsea cables reaching to the world. The vision becomes pan-African, then global. Ends with CTA: "Join the ecosystem."
"Africa's shared backbone. The infrastructure enabler for a connected continent."

Strengths

  • Leads with the problem ISPs already feel (fragmentation, multiple contracts)
  • The "unification moment" is emotionally powerful — disorder to order is universally satisfying
  • Infrastructure partners see their assets becoming more useful
  • Investors see the coordination thesis immediately
  • Technically feasible — 2D/2.5D map with particle effects, doesn't require heavy 3D

Risks

  • Map-centric approach can feel generic (many infra companies use maps)
  • Stays at macro level — doesn't show the product or what the ISP actually does
  • The "problem first" opening may read negative for the first few seconds
  • Needs strong copy to differentiate from a generic "we connect Africa" message

ISP Gut Check

An ISP visiting this page would immediately recognize their own reality — multiple providers, fragmented contracts, infrastructure that doesn't coordinate. The unification moment positions Share as the answer to a problem they live with daily. Risk: they might think "another network company with a map" if the visual execution isn't distinctive enough.

2

The ISP Story

One partner plugs in — watch their world expand

Product-forward, clean data visualization, Stripe-like precision

Small (one ISP) to massive (ecosystem). The partner is the hero.

Limited → connected → empowered → part of something massive

"Plug in. Keep your customers. Grow without limits."

Scroll Storyboard
One small ISP
limited reach
Beat 1 — The Partner (Before)
A single ISP. Small coverage radius. One expensive provider connection. A few users. A modest router icon. Feels constrained, isolated. The ISP visitor sees themselves here.
"You've built a business. You serve your community. But growing means more contracts, more capital, more risk."
The plug-in moment
Beat 2 — The Connection
A Share node appears below the ISP. A single elegant connection forms — the "plug in" moment. Simple, clean. This is the Stripe analogy made visual: one integration, not an overhaul. The action feels easy, almost trivial.
"One API connection. That's it. No civil works. No new contracts. No upfront fees."
Coverage explodes
backbone unlocked
Beat 3 — The Expansion
Instant transformation. Coverage radius expands 10x. The single provider line becomes a thick backbone connecting to 12 infrastructure providers. User dots multiply. Revenue line climbs. The ISP's world just got massively bigger — without changing their business.
"Unlimited backhaul. Zero upfront commits. 12 infrastructure providers through one contract. Your business, amplified."
Financial layer —
payments + growth tools
Beat 4 — The Financial Layer
Zoom into the platform. A clean dashboard shows payments flowing — automatically split between partner and Share. Revenue tracker, wallet balance, interest accruing. This isn't just bandwidth — it's a financial ecosystem that grows with you.
"Every payment, automatically split. Your revenue, your wallet, your growth tools. We only earn when you earn."
Ecosystem pullback —
dozens of partners
Beat 5 — The Ecosystem
Pull back to reveal dozens of ISPs all connected to Share. Each with their own expanding coverage bubble. The ecosystem is alive, growing. The message: you're not alone, you're joining something already in motion.
"Join an ecosystem that's already live, already growing. The more partners join, the stronger everyone gets."

Strengths

  • ISP is literally the hero of the story — maximum identification for the #1 audience
  • Shows the product journey: before/after is the most persuasive sales structure
  • Naturally introduces the financial layer (not just bandwidth)
  • The "plug in" moment directly addresses integration fear — it looks easy
  • Works as a validator AND door-opener simultaneously

Risks

  • May feel too B2B-focused for investors and end users watching the same page
  • Less "wow" factor — more product demo than immersive experience
  • Doesn't lean into the physical infrastructure as heavily (subsea cables, data centers)
  • Could feel more like a landing page than a brand statement

ISP Gut Check

This is the direction an ISP would most directly respond to. They see themselves, they see the transformation, they see the ease of joining. The before/after is the single most effective sales visual for this audience. Risk: other audiences (investors, governments) may feel it's too narrow — but the ISP page could carry the detailed version while the homepage uses a lighter touch.

3

The Collapse

Watch unnecessary complexity dissolve into elegant simplicity

Architectural, almost mechanical. Satisfying compression. Think exploded diagram in reverse.

Tall/complex structure compresses into flat/elegant as you scroll. ASMR-like satisfaction.

Overwhelm → recognition → compression → relief → clarity

"One contract. One network. One financial layer. Everything else was noise."

Scroll Storyboard
The current reality:
layers of middlemen
Beat 1 — The Complexity
Exploded view of the current internet supply chain. Subsea cable at the bottom, carrier, reseller, regional provider, local ISP at the top. Each layer has its own contract floating beside it. Lines between layers are tangled, inefficient. It looks exhausting because it IS exhausting.
"You're 400km from the sea. Between you and bandwidth: 4 middlemen, 5 contracts, and a mountain of upfront capital."
Cost stacking
at every layer
Beat 2 — The Cost
Zoom into the layers. Each one adds cost: upfront commits, idle capacity fees, separate billing systems, contract overhead. Red cost labels stack up visually. The system is heavy, expensive, and fragile. An ISP feels this in their bones.
"Every layer takes a cut. Every contract locks capital. Every provider operates in isolation. The cost compounds — and the end user pays for all of it."
Layers compress
into Share
Beat 3 — The Collapse
Share enters. The layers begin compressing — physically squeezing together as you scroll. Contracts dissolve and merge into one. The satisfying visual of complexity folding into simplicity. Red turns to blue/green. Five layers become one clean bar.
"Share sits between the infrastructure and you. One contract. Every provider. No commits."
Clean architecture
one layer does it all
Beat 4 — The New Architecture
The result: a clean, flat architecture. ISP at top, Share in the middle, all 12 infrastructure providers at the bottom accessed through one layer. Breathtakingly simple. The contrast with Beat 1 is the entire argument.
"Unlimited backhaul. 12 providers. One integration. No upfront fees. We only earn when you earn."
Scale — many ISPs,
one shared backbone
Beat 5 — The Scale
Zoom out. The clean architecture now spans across Africa with dozens of ISPs plugged in on top, each serving thousands of users. The single Share layer carries them all. Numbers fade in. The vision: one shared backbone for an entire continent.
"Africa's shared backbone. The more networks join, the stronger everyone gets. Join the ecosystem."

Strengths

  • The before/after of complexity vs. simplicity is instantly legible — even non-technical viewers get it
  • Directly addresses the "what's the catch?" objection by showing how simple it actually is
  • The supply chain visual matches Luis's own analogy (ISP 400km from the sea)
  • Satisfying compression animation is memorable and shareable
  • Works well on mobile — vertical scroll maps naturally to vertical supply chain

Risks

  • The "problem first" opening is even more explicit here — could feel negative for 3-5 seconds
  • More diagrammatic than cinematic — may not deliver the "WOW" factor Share wants
  • Doesn't show physical infrastructure (cables, data centers) — it's abstract/architectural
  • May feel like an explainer rather than a brand experience

ISP Gut Check

This direction speaks directly to ISPs who are currently managing multiple contracts and paying upfront commits. Watching their exact pain point get elegantly solved on screen is viscerally satisfying. Best direction for converting an ISP who already understands the industry. Less effective for someone who doesn't know what backhaul is.

4

The Light Journey

Follow a data packet from ocean floor to living room — but the story is human

Cinematic, literal 3D infrastructure with human moments at each stop

Linear journey through fiber. Camera follows light. Pauses at each node to reveal impact.

Wonder → technical awe → human connection → scale reveal

"From the ocean floor to your living room — this is what coordination looks like."

Scroll Storyboard
Subsea cable —
where it all begins
Beat 1 — The Origin
Deep underwater. A subsea cable on the ocean floor off Mombasa. A pulse of light — a data packet — is born. It begins its journey. The scale is vast, quiet, almost meditative. You feel the weight of the infrastructure that already exists.
"7 subsea cables connect Africa to the world. The bandwidth is here. It just needs a way to reach everyone."
Data center —
routing & intelligence
Beat 2 — The Node
The light enters a data center. Camera reveals racks, routers, transceivers — literal infrastructure (reference photos from Share will inform this). The packet pauses, gets routed intelligently. This is where Share's coordination layer lives. Brief flash of the platform dashboard — routing decisions being made.
"10 data centers. Every packet routed intelligently. Share's coordination layer decides the fastest, cheapest, most reliable path."
Fiber backbone —
light at speed of light
Beat 3 — The Speed
Inside the fiber. Close-up. Multiple strands of light racing in parallel — this is the moment that delivers "internet is COOL." The speed, the glass, the light. Pure physics made visual. 500+ km of this, connecting communities that were previously isolated.
"Light. Moving at the speed of light. Through glass. 500+ kilometers of shared backbone, connecting what was never connected before."
Last mile —
wired to wireless
Beat 4 — The Last Mile
The light exits fiber and climbs a cell tower. Signal radiates outward. Nearby, fiber connects directly to buildings. The transition from backbone to last-mile delivery. This is where the ISP partner lives — this is their domain, and Share just made it vastly more powerful.
"The last mile is yours. Share powers everything behind it — so you can reach further, faster, for less."
Home — real people,
real internet
Beat 5 — The Destination
The packet arrives. A person at home, streaming, working, connecting. The abstract journey becomes tangible and human. Then: pull back. Millions of packets moving simultaneously. Africa lit up. The full network revealed. "Share is here. For everyone."
"8 million people in our coverage area. Every one of them deserves fast, reliable internet. Share makes it possible."

Strengths

  • Closest to Share's own creative brief — validates their thinking while adding the human layer
  • Delivers the "internet is COOL / light through glass" moment they explicitly want
  • Physical infrastructure (cables, DCs, towers) all showcased — builds credibility
  • Works for ALL audiences — ISPs see their domain, investors see scale, users see themselves
  • The human moments at each stop prevent it from being a dry infrastructure tour

Risks

  • Heavy 3D — most technically demanding and expensive to build. Performance risk on Android/slow connections.
  • Linear journey may feel passive — you're watching, not discovering
  • Doesn't show the business model or financial layer (it's a physics story, not a business story)
  • The ISP's specific pain points (contracts, capital, fragmentation) aren't addressed until they leave the homepage

ISP Gut Check

Beautiful and impressive, but an ISP might think "cool animation" without understanding what Share does FOR THEM. The infrastructure credibility is high (they see real cables, real towers), but the business case is implicit. Best paired with a strong text overlay layer that tells the ISP story while the visual tells the physics story. Otherwise risks being a "wow, and?" experience.

5

The Ecosystem Machine

A living system where every connection makes the whole network stronger

Generative, organic-mechanical. Like watching a circuit board grow like a plant.

Growth simulation. Each scroll adds a node, and the whole system reconfigures and gets stronger.

Seed → growth → network effects → inevitability → "I want in"

"Every partner that joins makes the entire network stronger. The ecosystem is already growing."

Scroll Storyboard
Single point of light
Mombasa
Beat 1 — The Seed
A single glowing point. Mombasa. This is where Share started — one data center, one subsea cable connection, one idea. The screen is almost entirely dark. The point pulses gently, alive with potential.
"It started with a single connection in Mombasa. One data center. One idea: what if Africa's infrastructure worked as one?"
First tendrils —
fiber reaching out
Beat 2 — The Growth
Tendrils of fiber reach outward from the seed. Organic, almost biological — but clearly technological. Each tendril finds a node (a data center, a tower, an ISP). Connections form. The system is growing — not because Share is building everything, but because it's connecting what already exists.
"Share doesn't build new infrastructure. It connects what already exists — aggregating from 12 providers into one shared backbone."
ISP joins —
whole network strengthens
Beat 3 — The Network Effect
An ISP node (in amber) connects to the network. The moment it plugs in, a visible ripple propagates through the ENTIRE system — every node gets slightly brighter, every connection gets slightly stronger. The ISP's coverage area expands. This is the network effect made visible: your growth IS the ecosystem's growth.
"Every partner that joins makes the entire network stronger. More coverage. More redundancy. More value for everyone."
Critical mass —
ecosystem alive
Beat 4 — Critical Mass
The network has reached critical mass. Dozens of nodes — infrastructure (red), ISPs (amber), financial flows (green) — all pulsing with activity. Data packets stream everywhere. The system looks alive, self-sustaining. A subtle green layer appears: the financial ecosystem flowing beneath everything. Two layers, one system.
"Backbone + payment gateway. Two layers. One ecosystem. Growing every day."
Full vision —
"there's a spot for you"
Beat 5 — The Invitation
Pull back to continental scale. The ecosystem spans Africa — dense, active, growing. But there's a visible gap, an empty node with a dashed outline. The final frame is an invitation: this ecosystem is growing, and there's a spot for you. CTA: "Join Share."
"The ecosystem is live. Growing every day. There's a spot for you. Join Share."

Strengths

  • The "your spot is waiting" ending is a powerful CTA — creates FOMO and agency simultaneously
  • Network effect visualization is unique — differentiates from generic infra animations
  • Growth from seed to continent is emotionally compelling (startup → scale narrative)
  • Naturally shows that Share is already live and growing — addresses trust and brand awareness
  • The ripple effect (one ISP joining strengthens everyone) is the core business model made visual

Risks

  • More abstract than literal — doesn't show physical infrastructure (cables, racks, towers)
  • Could feel like any network/marketplace animation if not executed distinctively
  • Doesn't explain what Share actually does operationally (no product, no financial layer detail)
  • The organic/generative style may clash with the "tech-forward, infrastructure reliability" brand they want

ISP Gut Check

The "join an ecosystem that's already growing" message is strong for ISPs who worry about being early adopters. Seeing other ISPs already in the network reduces the trust barrier. The dashed-outline "your spot" ending is the strongest CTA of all five directions. But the ISP needs to understand what they're joining — this direction needs a clear product explanation alongside the growth visual.
