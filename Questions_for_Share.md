# Questions for Share

### **PRODUCT & BUSINESS**

1. How do you pitch Share when you're talking to an investor vs. an ISP partner vs. someone on the street? We want to hear it in your words so we can build the messaging from how you naturally talk about it.
2. Can you walk us through how Share actually works from an ISP partner's perspective? From first contact to being live on the network, what does that journey look like?
3. What's the single biggest problem you solve for ISPs that nobody else does?
4. The "no upfront capacity fees" model is a strong differentiator. How do ISP partners usually react when they hear that for the first time?
5. How do you explain the hyperscale model to someone non-technical? We'll need to translate this into visuals and copy that lands with all three audiences.
6. Where is Share in 2-3 years? More countries, more services, more infrastructure? Understanding the trajectory helps us design a site that scales with you.
7. How many ISP partners are currently active on the network, and how fast is that growing?
8. You mention future financial services (lending, FOREX). How central is that to the long-term vision, and should the website hint at it?

**BRAND & IDENTITY**

1. Your current brand guidelines are in place, but you're open to a subtle refresh. What feels right about the current brand, and what feels like it no longer fits?
2. What's the one thing you want people to remember after visiting the website?
3. You mentioned references like terminal.industries, Stripe, Cloudflare, Apple, Starlink. What specifically draws you to each of these? Is it the visual feel, the storytelling, the way they present data, or something else?

**AUDIENCE & PRIORITIES**

1. Of your three audiences (investors, ISP partners, end consumers), who is the most important right now? If we had to prioritize the experience for one, which one?
2. For the Series A, what are investors most skeptical about, and what do they get most excited about? That will help us know what to lead with.
3. For ISP partners, what's the biggest hesitation they have before joining the Share ecosystem? What usually gets them over the line?
4. The D2C layer is listed as "coming soon" in the brief. How real is this timeline? Should we design it as a placeholder or as a full experience?

**COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE**

1. Who do you consider your closest competitors, and what are they doing well or poorly with their digital presence?
2. How do you want Share to feel compared to other players in the African connectivity space? More tech-forward? More human? More institutional?
3. Is there any company outside of your industry whose brand or website you admire and would love to be compared to?
4. Is there any company outside of Africa that is offering similar services as you?

**STORY & CONTENT**

1. What's the narrative arc you imagine as someone scrolls through the homepage?
2. You have incredible numbers (7 subsea cables, 400+ km fiber, 8M+ people covered, 7 micro data centers). Are all of these confirmed and usable on the site, or are some still evolving?
3. Are there any ISP partner testimonials or case studies we can use? Even short quotes would go a long way on the B2B page.
4. Do you have any content ready (written copy, bios, partner logos, investor logos, press coverage), or will that need to be produced alongside the design?

**3D & IMMERSIVE DIRECTION**

1. When you think about the 3D experience, do you imagine something abstract and atmospheric (light particles, data flowing through networks), or more literal and physical (actual data centers, cables, server rooms)? Or both?
2. How important is it that the 3D elements represent real Share infrastructure vs. a more conceptual visualization of connectivity and scale?
3. Did anything specific about Lightweight catch your attention that you'd want to carry into this project?

**TECHNICAL**

1. On our end, we'd build this in React/Next.js with Sanity as the CMS. Are you comfortable with that stack, or is there anything on your side that would require a different approach?
2. For hosting, we'd recommend Vercel. It pairs well with Next.js, handles global CDN delivery out of the box, and keeps performance tight. Does that work for you, or do you have an existing hosting setup you'd prefer to use?
3. Are there any third-party integrations we should plan for? CRM, analytics, email marketing, chatbots, demo booking tools?
4. The brief mentions a "Get Started" flow and demo requests. Where do those leads currently go, and what should happen after someone submits a form?
5. For the coverage map and "Find a Provider" feature, is there an existing data source or API we'd pull from, or does that need to be built from scratch?
6. Are there any performance benchmarks beyond the sub-3-second load time? Specific Lighthouse scores, Core Web Vitals targets?
7. Who handles the site post-launch on your side? The CMS (Sanity) is very intuitive for non-technical users, but it helps to know who'll be managing content day to day. We would give your team a workshop.

**PRACTICAL / PROCESS**

1. Who from your team will be involved in feedback and approvals?
2. Any hard deadlines we should know about? Events, investor meetings, product launches that the site needs to be ready for?
3. Are there any legal or compliance considerations around how you present metrics, partnerships, or financial information on the site?
4. On the admin side, once we align on the direction and budget, we'd move to signing a contract and collecting a deposit so we can lock in the timeline and get started.
